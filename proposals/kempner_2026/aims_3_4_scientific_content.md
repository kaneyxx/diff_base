## Aim 3 — Cross-stain spatial-grounding generalization

> **Suggested framing in the May-14 draft (verbatim):**
> *"Cross-stain spatial-grounding generalization, i.e., does the spatial
> CoT reward survive when the model is trained on H&E but reasoned over
> a virtual IHC channel produced by a diffusion model?"*

### 3.1 Scientific framing
- **Working title.** *Cross-stain spatial-grounding generalization:
  testing whether COSMO+ spatial CoT supervision survives the H&E → virtual
  IHC channel switch.*
- **Single falsifiable hypothesis.** When the COSMO+ reasoning substrate
  is switched from H&E patch tokens to **virtual IHC patch tokens**
  generated by a FLUX.1-Kontext H&E→IHC diffusion model, the spatial-grounding
  reward signal (Aim 2: **IoU@10**) and concept-grounding signal (Aim 2:
  **CGS**) are preserved: **ΔIoU@10 ≤ 0.05 absolute** and **ΔCGS ≤ 3
  points** on the TCGA pan-cancer eval pool, averaged across 16 of the
  32 solid-tumor cohorts (the 16 with non-trivial cross-stain biology;
  see § 3.2).
- **Link to Aim 1 / Aim 2.** Direct stress-test of Aim 1's recipe and
  Aim 2's interpretability harness. The CONCH pseudo-label pipeline that
  Aim 1 relies on is **rebuilt on a virtual IHC channel** rather than
  H&E; if the IoU@k / CGS / HR metrics from Aim 2 still hold under the
  modality switch, the spatial-grounding signal is **modality-portable**
  (a falsifiable claim that the main draft cannot test on H&E alone).
- **Why now (gap statement).** The closest prior method is
  **VirtualMultiplexer** (Pati et al., *Nature Machine Intelligence* 6,
  593–606, 2024), which validates virtual IF utility for downstream cell
  classifiers but not for downstream **vision-language reasoning**. The
  COSMO+ interpretability harness (Aim 2 in the main draft) is the
  first opportunity to ask whether the spatial CoT reward is **modality
  invariant**. The gap is the absence of any cross-stain reasoning-benchmark
  result in the literature — every published WSI-VLM (SlideChat,
  WSI-LLaVA, PathChat-Plus) reasons over H&E only.

### 3.2 Data
- **Training corpus for the diffusion model (Aim 3 / Aim 4 share).**
  - **ORION** (Lin et al., *Cell* 186, 363–381, 2023): 40 colorectal
    patients, 13 paired same-section IF channels, on disk at
    `/n/scratch/users/f/fas994/bao/data/`. Used for the dense-panel,
    same-section diffusion training that yields the strongest in-distribution
    H&E→IF prior.
  - **BCI** (Liu et al., CVPRW 2022): 9 648 paired serial-section H&E ↔
    HER2 IHC tiles at 1024², CC-BY-NC-SA. Adds breast tissue and
    serial-section robustness.
  - Pre-tile to 512² training crops via the existing
    `MultiPatientBiomarkerDataset` (ORION) and `src/data/dataset.py` (BCI).
- **Evaluation corpus (no diffusion training).**
  - **TCGA** pan-cancer slides identical to Aim 1/2 (the existing CONCH
    feature lake on FASRC, 11 513 PM + 18 161 FS slides, 32 cohorts).
    Aim 3 runs **diffusion inference** to generate a virtual IHC channel
    per slide tile for the eval subset.
- **Patient and image counts.**
  - Diffusion training: 40 ORION + 4 K BCI patients/cases ≈ 4 K paired
    samples, ~2 M tile pairs after augmentation.
  - Diffusion eval (TCGA): one virtual IHC tile per CONCH patch on
    the SlideBench-VQA-TCGA + WSI-Bench eval splits (~1.2 M tiles
    across 16 cohorts).
- **Paired vs unpaired; same- vs serial-section.** ORION = paired
  same-section; BCI = paired serial-section. We deliberately train on
  both to make the diffusion prior robust to both regimes; serial-section
  ablation is reported.
- **Target IHC marker(s).**
  - From ORION: CD45, CD8a, CD20, FOXP3, PD-L1, Ki67 (6 panel) —
    chosen for cross-cancer relevance.
  - From BCI: HER2 (single marker, breast-specific).
  - The diffusion model is **marker-conditional** (1-of-7 learnable
    embedding) so any of the 7 channels can be requested at inference.
- **Tissue type(s) covered (training).** Colorectal (ORION) + breast
  (BCI). Tissue type(s) at eval: all 32 TCGA cohorts (zero-shot
  generalisation).
- **Train / validation / test split.**
  - Diffusion training: patient-disjoint 5-fold CV within ORION; case-disjoint
    within BCI (per BCI's official split).
  - Downstream COSMO+ eval: TCGA splits identical to Aim 1/2 — no overlap
    between Aim 3 diffusion training and TCGA reasoning eval.
- **Data security level.** **DSL 1**. ORION (Synapse, CC-BY-4.0), BCI
  (research-only, level 1), TCGA (NCI GDC public). No PHI; matches the
  main draft's posture.
- **Required preprocessing.**
  - Tile generation: ORION already tiled; BCI is tiled in the source
    release.
  - Macenko stain normalisation on H&E inputs (already in
    `src/data/transforms.py`).
  - Per-channel min-max + p99 clipping on IF/IHC outputs.
  - Latent caching via `src/data/cache.py::LatentCache` to keep VAE off
    the critical path.
  - For TCGA inference: align CONCH patch coordinates with the diffusion
    output grid (CONCH is 256-px patches; diffusion is 512²
    centred-crop). Sliding-window inference with Hann-window blending.

### 3.3 Model
- **Backbone.** FLUX.1-Kontext-dev, 12 B-parameter DiT
  (`black-forest-labs/FLUX.1-Kontext-dev`); see
  `src/models/flux/v1/transformer.py`. 19 joint + 38 single blocks,
  hidden dim 3072, 24 heads, latent ch 16, RoPE axes (16, 56, 56).
- **Conditioning mechanism.** Kontext-style **sequence-wise concatenation**
  of reference (H&E) and target (IHC) token streams; stream-id 1.0 for
  reference, 0.0 for target. Marker identity is encoded both as a text
  prompt token (`"<marker> immunohistochemistry of paired H&E section"`)
  and as a 1-of-7 learnable additive embedding on the timestep stream.
  Code path: `src/models/flux/v1/conditioning.py::prepare_kontext_conditioning`.
- **Pretrained weights to be used.** FLUX.1-Kontext-dev (open weights);
  T5-XXL + CLIP-L text encoders frozen and embedding-cached (zero GPU
  residency, see § hardware notes in `scal_lora_template.yaml`); FLUX
  VAE frozen. **Only the DiT transformer is trainable** via LoRA.
- **Sampling resolution and patch size.** 512² training crops; latent
  64² × 16ch. Inference at 512² for COSMO+ evaluation (matches CONCH
  patch grid coarsening); 1024² with tile blending as visualisation.
- **Inference sampler.** Flow-matching Euler with 28 steps,
  guidance_scale 2.5 (Kontext default). 50-step DPM-Solver++ as
  appendix robustness check.
- **Training schedule.**
  - **Fine-tune** (not from scratch). LoRA rank 32 / alpha 32 /
    dropout 0.0 (matches our rank-sweep readiness data showing this is
    the throughput / quality sweet spot at 4×A100).
  - Target modules: Q, K, V, O, FFN proj across all 19 joint + 38 single
    blocks (534 LoRA tensors).
  - Training budget: 30 epochs across ORION+BCI ≈ 80 k optimizer steps
    on 4×A100 (4 GPU × bs 2 × accum 1).
  - **AdamW** β = (0.9, 0.999), wd 0.01, 8-bit optimizer via bitsandbytes.
  - LR 1 × 10⁻⁴ with **warmup_steps = 500** (longer than Aim 1's
    LLM warmup; diffusion is sensitive). Fallback LR 1 × 10⁻⁶
    documented in case bf16 DDP grad-sync triggers NaN
    (see Risk § 3.8 — already observed in the readiness sweep).
  - bf16 mixed precision throughout.

### 3.4 Baselines
- **Non-diffusion baselines.**
  - **CycleGAN** (Zhu et al., ICCV 2017): unpaired stain translation on
    the same ORION+BCI mix.
  - **Pix2pix-HD** (Wang et al., CVPR 2018): paired, 1024² resolution.
  - **StainGAN** (Shaban et al., ISBI 2019): single-marker baseline.
  - **VirtualMultiplexer** (Pati et al., *Nat. Machine Intelligence* 2024):
    re-trained on ORION+BCI from authors' public code.
- **Diffusion baselines.**
  - **PathLDM** (Yellapragada et al., CVPR 2024): text-conditioned latent
    diffusion for histopathology, adapted with our marker prompts.
  - **BCI-Diff / IHCDiff** (Yan et al., MICCAI 2024): diffusion variant
    of the BCI baseline, re-trained on ORION+BCI.
  - **ASCS** (Liu et al., MICCAI 2024): adaptive supervised diffusion
    for IHC.
- **In-house ablations.**
  - Diffusion rank sweep (LoRA 8 / 16 / 32 / 64) — data already collected
    in `04_scalability_testing/`.
  - Marker-conditional vs marker-unconditional diffusion model
    (does the 1-of-7 embedding actually buy anything?).
  - **Reasoning-substrate ablation.** Run the COSMO+ Aim 1/2 evaluation
    on (i) H&E channel only, (ii) virtual IHC channel only,
    (iii) H&E + virtual IHC concatenated. The Aim 3 hypothesis is
    tested specifically on (ii) vs (i).

### 3.5 Evaluation
- **Primary outcome metric.** **Δ IoU@10** between (i) H&E-grounded
  COSMO+ from Aim 1 and (ii) virtual-IHC-grounded COSMO+ re-trained
  with the same recipe on virtual IHC patch tokens. Averaged across
  16 TCGA cohorts × ≥ 200 spatial-grounding questions each.
- **Secondary metrics.**
  - **Δ Concept Grounding Score (CGS).**
  - **Δ Reasoning Fidelity Score (RFS).**
  - **Hallucination Rate (HR)** per cancer cohort.
  - **Diffusion-side fidelity controls**: FID-50k per marker,
    MS-SSIM, biomarker-IoU @ Otsu (so we can argue any drop in
    downstream IoU@10 is not driven by trivially bad diffusion outputs).
- **Pathologist validation plan.**
  - **Two** GI pathologists + **one** breast pathologist (1 senior, 2
    junior), consented through the Sorger / Yu lab pathology
    consortium.
  - 50 cases per cancer × 3 cancers (CRC, breast, lung) = 150 reads
    per pathologist. Random presentation, real-or-generated label
    hidden. 5-point Likert on **diagnostic plausibility of the virtual
    IHC view** + **clinical correctness of the COSMO+ chain that cites
    it**.
  - Cross-rater κ + per-cancer aggregated Likert.
- **Downstream-task evaluation.** Yes — the COSMO+ pipeline **is** the
  downstream task. Aim 3 is a controlled ablation on the spatial-grounding
  reward channel.

### 3.8 Risk and fallback
- **Risk 1: cross-stain domain shift breaks generalisation to TCGA.**
  Mitigation: train on ORION (same-section) AND BCI (serial-section)
  jointly so the model sees both regimes. If still bad, fall back to a
  **per-cancer** small adaptation set drawn from each TCGA cohort's
  diagnostic notes (BCI for breast cohorts, ORION-style for CRC
  cohorts).
- **Risk 2: bf16 + DDP grad-sync produces NaN loss at warmup.**
  Observed in the readiness sweep (job 40052101). Workaround applied:
  warmup_steps=200 → 500 and fallback LR=1 × 10⁻⁶. The NaN does **not**
  invalidate throughput / memory / utilisation numbers used in the
  technical-readiness section.
- **Risk 3: TCGA inference at 1.2 M tiles exceeds the buffer.**
  Mitigation: report on a sampled subset (300 k tiles → 8 GPU-hours)
  and document the sampling protocol. The hypothesis is testable on
  this subset.
- **Hard fallback.** If FLUX.1-Kontext does not converge on the joint
  ORION+BCI corpus, fall back to ORION-only with the **CycleGAN**
  baseline as the virtual IHC channel. Report as a negative result
  with the cross-stain question answered for GAN-grade virtual IHC
  rather than diffusion-grade virtual IHC. Manuscript scope shrinks
  but the falsifiable test still completes.

## Aim 4 — IHC-as-pseudo-label upgrade for tumor-marker subtype questions

> **Suggested framing in the May-14 draft (verbatim):**
> *"IHC-as-pseudo-label upgrade, i.e., does an IHC-conditioned CoT
> trace reduce hallucination rate on tumor-marker subtypes (e.g., HER2
> in breast, PD-L1 in lung)?"*

### 4.1 Scientific framing
- **Working title.** *IHC-conditioned chain-of-thought traces lower
  COSMO+ hallucination rate on tumor-marker subtype questions.*
- **Single falsifiable hypothesis.** Augmenting the CONCH text-similarity
  pseudo-label stream of Aim 1 with **virtual-IHC-derived marker-positive
  region pseudo-labels** (produced by the Aim 3 diffusion model) lowers
  the COSMO+ **Hallucination Rate** on three pre-registered
  marker-conditional question pools — **HER2 in breast (TCGA-BRCA),
  PD-L1 in lung (TCGA-LUAD), MMR/MSI in colorectal (TCGA-COAD/READ)** —
  by **≥ 25 % relative** vs the Aim 1 CONCH-only baseline, at
  paired-bootstrap p < 0.01, without statistically harming Hallucination
  Rate on non-marker-conditional questions in the same cohorts.
- **Link to Aim 1 / Aim 2.** Direct upgrade of Aim 1's pseudo-label
  pipeline. The Aim 1 reward stack has four signals
  (format, diagnostic, spatial, concept); Aim 4 introduces a **fifth
  signal** — **marker-conditional grounding** — only for the three
  cancer-marker subtype subsets. Aim 4 is then evaluated using Aim 2's
  Hallucination Rate metric, ensuring metric continuity across the
  whole proposal.
- **Why now (gap statement).** Tumor-marker subtype questions
  (e.g., HER2 status from H&E alone) are an active hallucination
  failure mode of every published WSI-VLM (SlideChat, WSI-LLaVA,
  PathChat-Plus). Real IHC slides are not always available at scan
  time; **virtual IHC** is the only scalable way to inject
  marker-level evidence into the reasoning pipeline. No prior work
  measures the effect of *virtual* IHC pseudo-labels on a VLM's
  hallucination rate.

### 4.2 Data
- **No additional diffusion training data** — Aim 4 reuses the Aim 3
  marker-conditional FLUX.1-Kontext weights.
- **Question pools (pre-registered).**
  - HER2 in **TCGA-BRCA**: ~200 questions extracted from the
    WSI-Bench breast subset and the SlideBench-VQA-TCGA breast subset,
    filtered by `mentions(HER2|ERBB2)` regex.
  - PD-L1 in **TCGA-LUAD**: ~200 questions filtered by
    `mentions(PD-L1|CD274|PDL1)`.
  - MMR/MSI in **TCGA-COAD + TCGA-READ**: ~200 questions filtered by
    `mentions(MMR|MSI|microsatellite|MLH1|MSH2|MSH6|PMS2)`.
  - **Control pool**: 200 non-marker-conditional questions per cohort
    (anatomy, grade, tumor type) — used to verify no harm.
- **Ground truth source for marker status.** TCGA-CDR clinical data
  resource (Liu et al., *Cell* 173, 400–416, 2018) for HER2 status
  and MMR/MSI status; PD-L1 status from CPTAC-LUAD where available
  (~40 % coverage; rest annotated by trained reader on TCGA-LUAD
  diagnostic slides).
- **Data security level.** **DSL 1**. TCGA-CDR is public; CPTAC-LUAD
  is public; the diagnostic-slide PD-L1 read is internal to the
  pathologist consortium but reports counts/proportions only — no
  PHI.
- **Required preprocessing.** Question pool extraction
  (regex + manual filtering by clinical reader); marker-positive
  region pseudo-labels are derived from the virtual IHC channel by
  Otsu thresholding on the marker-specific output, exactly mirroring
  the existing CONCH pseudo-label pipeline (so the change to the
  Aim 1 reward computation is one new input stream, not a redesign).

### 4.3 Model
- **Diffusion backbone (re-used).** Aim 3 weights, no further fine-tune.
- **Pseudo-label pipeline upgrade.**
  - For each TCGA tile, run virtual-IHC inference for the relevant
    marker (HER2 in BRCA, PD-L1 in LUAD, MMR-MSI proxy in COAD/READ).
    The MMR/MSI virtual marker is the model's prediction for the
    **TIL pattern** that distinguishes MSI-high tumors — based on
    the FOXP3 / CD8a / CD45 mix already in the ORION panel, not a
    direct "MMR/MSI stain" (no such stain exists; this is an
    immune-microenvironment proxy. Documented limitation.)
  - Otsu-threshold the marker output → marker-positive regions.
  - These regions become the **marker-conditional pseudo-labels** for
    the Aim 1 reward signal #5 (`marker_grounding`).
- **COSMO+ training schedule (this Aim).** Add **one extra epoch** of
  curriculum SFT on the marker-conditional question subsets, with the
  marker-grounding reward enabled. No change to the architecture or
  LoRA rank from Aim 1; the only new piece is the additional
  reward-signal channel.

### 4.4 Baselines
- **CONCH-only pseudo-label baseline** (Aim 1 as published in the main
  draft). This is the **head-to-head** comparison for the falsifiable
  test.
- **Real-IHC pseudo-label oracle** (where real IHC is available — BCI
  subset of TCGA-BRCA, CPTAC-LUAD with available PD-L1 reads).
  Upper-bound check: virtual IHC should close ≥ 50 % of the gap
  between CONCH-only and real-IHC.
- **Random-mask control.** Inject random marker-positive regions
  (same area distribution) as a sham pseudo-label. If the random
  control also lowers HR, our effect is confounded by reward
  regularisation rather than marker information.
- **Adversarial virtual-IHC control.** Generate virtual IHC for the
  **wrong** marker (e.g., feed PD-L1 conditioning when the question
  is HER2). The effect should disappear if HR is sensitive to
  marker identity.

### 4.5 Evaluation
- **Primary outcome metric.** **Δ Hallucination Rate** (Aim 2
  definition) on marker-conditional questions, paired-bootstrap
  p < 0.01, target ≥ 25 % relative reduction.
- **Secondary metrics.**
  - **Δ Concept Grounding Score** on the same marker-conditional
    questions.
  - **HR on the control pool** (non-marker-conditional questions in
    the same cohorts) — must not increase.
  - **Marker-status classification AUROC** as a single-number
    summary of "does the model actually know HER2/PD-L1/MMR
    status now?".
  - **Calibration**: ECE of confidence vs accuracy, with and
    without the marker-conditional pseudo-label channel.
- **Pathologist validation plan.**
  - Three readers (breast, GI, thoracic).
  - 30 cases per marker × 3 markers = 90 reads per pathologist.
  - Reads include both **virtual IHC visual plausibility** (Likert
    1–5) and **clinical correctness of the COSMO+ reasoning chain
    that cites it**.
  - Cross-rater κ.
- **Downstream-task evaluation.** This Aim **is** the downstream eval
  — Aim 4 closes the loop from Aim 1's reward signals through Aim 3's
  diffusion model back to Aim 2's hallucination metric.

### 4.6 Compute and storage
- **GPU-hours within the 22-day buffer.**
  - Virtual IHC inference for ~30 k marker-conditional tiles across
    3 cohorts × 3 markers: 30 k × 28 steps × 0.4 s / 4 GPU
    ≈ **~25 GPU-hours**.
  - Aim 1 curriculum-SFT extra epoch on marker-conditional subsets
    (~50 k Q/A across 3 cohorts × 5 s/step on 4 GPU): **~70 GPU-hours**.
  - Baseline / ablation passes (CONCH-only, random-mask, adversarial):
    ~30 GPU-hours total.
  - **Aim 4 total ≈ 120 GPU-hours on A100 80GB (≈ 65 on H100).**
- **GPU type preference.** A100 80GB (matches main draft + Aim 1
  recipe). H100 equivalent. L40S 48GB **acceptable** for Aim 4
  because the workload is the Qwen2.5-7B + LoRA stack (already
  validated on L40S in `04_scalability_testing/`, not the FLUX-12B
  workload).
- **Storage delta.** ~50 GB additional for the marker-conditional
  virtual IHC tiles + reward-stream caches. Subsumed under the
  Aim 3 +0.5 TB delta.

### 4.8 Risk and fallback
- **Risk 1: virtual IHC quality too low for HER2 / PD-L1 (markers not
  in ORION).** HER2 is in BCI; PD-L1 is in ORION's 13 channels (so
  this is in-distribution); MMR/MSI is the *only* marker without a
  direct IHC equivalent in our diffusion training data. Mitigation:
  ship Aim 4 with HER2 + PD-L1 in the primary table and **report
  MMR/MSI as a stretch goal** with the immune-microenvironment proxy
  caveat clearly stated.
- **Risk 2: hallucination drop is not statistically significant.**
  Mitigation: pre-register the primary endpoint and the
  three-marker analysis plan to avoid post-hoc cherry-picking; if
  the global HR delta is null but the per-marker delta is
  significant for HER2 only, we report the per-marker result and
  flag the negative pooled outcome.
- **Risk 3: marker-grounding reward signal accidentally over-fits to
  the diffusion model's failure modes** (e.g., the virtual IHC is
  systematically wrong on a tissue type, and the model is rewarded
  for matching the wrong thing). Mitigation: the **real-IHC
  oracle ablation** detects this directly; if oracle and virtual
  agree, the signal is sound; if they disagree, we recalibrate the
  threshold or drop the marker.
- **Hard fallback.** Report negative result on Aim 4 (no HR
  reduction); preserve the Aim 3 cross-stain spatial-grounding
  result as the publishable virtual-staining contribution. Defer
  the marker-conditional Aim to a follow-up R21 / F-series award.
