# Aims 3 & 4 — Diffusion-Based Virtual Staining: Proposal Report

The COSMO+ Pan-Cancer programme establishes a spatially grounded reasoning recipe across thirty-two TCGA solid-tumor cohorts (Aim 1) and the interpretability harness that audits it (Aim 2). The two secondary aims described here ask whether the same recipe is **modality-portable** and whether augmenting it with **visual marker-level evidence** lowers hallucination on tumor-marker subtype questions. Both aims share a diffusion virtual-staining backbone, sit within the 30-day Kempner allocation buffer, and rely entirely on public DSL-1 data, so they introduce no incremental data-security obligations beyond those already declared for Aims 1 and 2.

The combined compute footprint for Aims 3 and 4 is approximately 300 A100 GPU-hours (≈ 170 H100 GPU-hours), well inside the 22-day buffer documented in the main proposal.

## Aim 3 — Cross-stain spatial-grounding generalization

### Scientific framing

**Working title.** *Cross-stain spatial-grounding generalization: testing whether COSMO+ spatial chain-of-thought supervision survives the H&E → virtual IHC channel switch.*

**Falsifiable hypothesis.** When the COSMO+ reasoning substrate is switched from H&E patch tokens to virtual-IHC patch tokens generated by a FLUX.1-Kontext H&E→IHC diffusion model, the spatial-grounding reward signal (IoU@10) and the concept-grounding signal (CGS) are preserved: ΔIoU@10 ≤ 0.05 absolute and ΔCGS ≤ 3 points on the TCGA pan-cancer evaluation pool, averaged across 16 of the 32 solid-tumor cohorts (the subset whose disease biology routinely cites cell-type-specific markers).

**Link to Aim 1 / Aim 2.** This is a direct stress test of Aim 1's training recipe and Aim 2's interpretability harness. The CONCH pseudo-label pipeline that Aim 1 relies on is **rebuilt on a virtual IHC channel** rather than H&E; if the IoU@k, Concept Grounding Score, and Hallucination Rate from Aim 2 still hold under the modality switch, the spatial-grounding signal is modality-portable — a falsifiable claim that the main proposal cannot test on H&E alone.

**Gap in the literature.** The closest prior method is **VirtualMultiplexer** (Pati et al., *Nature Machine Intelligence* 2024), which validates virtual immunofluorescence utility for downstream cell classifiers but not for downstream vision-language reasoning. Every published whole-slide vision-language model — SlideChat, WSI-LLaVA, PathChat-Plus — reasons over H&E only. COSMO+ is the first opportunity to ask whether spatial chain-of-thought rewards are **modality invariant**, and Aim 3 supplies the falsifiable test.

### Data

**Training corpus for the diffusion model.**
- **ORION** (Lin et al., *Cell* 186, 363–381, 2023). 40 colorectal cancer patients, 13 paired same-section immunofluorescence channels (CD45, CD31, CD68, CD4, FOXP3, CD8a, CD45RO, CD20, PD-L1, CD3e, CD163, PD-1, Ki67). Public access via Sage Synapse, CC-BY 4.0.
- **BCI** (Liu et al., CVPRW 2022). 9 648 paired serial-section H&E ↔ HER2 IHC tiles at 1024² resolution. Public, research-only.

ORION supplies same-section paired supervision; BCI supplies serial-section paired supervision and broadens the tissue distribution. We deliberately train on both regimes to make the diffusion prior robust at inference time, when registration quality across H&E and IHC is unknown.

**Evaluation corpus.** TCGA — the same pan-cancer pool used by Aim 1/2 (32 solid-tumor cohorts; 11 513 diagnostic + 18 161 frozen-section slides). The diffusion model is applied **zero-shot** to TCGA H&E to generate a virtual IHC channel per tile, and COSMO+ is then re-trained and re-evaluated on the resulting virtual-IHC substrate.

**Patient and image counts.** Diffusion training: 40 ORION patients + ≈ 4 K BCI cases ≈ 2 M tile pairs after augmentation. Diffusion inference: one virtual-IHC tile per CONCH patch on the SlideBench-VQA-TCGA and WSI-Bench eval splits (≈ 1.2 M tiles across 16 evaluation cohorts).

**Target IHC markers.** Cross-cancer-relevant markers from ORION (CD45, CD8a, CD20, FOXP3, PD-L1, Ki67) plus HER2 from BCI. The diffusion model is marker-conditional, so any of the seven channels can be requested at inference.

**Train / validation / test split.** Diffusion training uses patient-disjoint 5-fold cross-validation within ORION and the BCI authors' official case-disjoint split. TCGA splits for COSMO+ evaluation are identical to those used in Aims 1 and 2; there is no overlap between Aim 3 diffusion training and the TCGA reasoning eval.

**Data security level.** DSL 1 throughout. ORION, BCI, and TCGA are public; no PHI is touched. This matches the data-security posture declared for Aims 1 and 2.

**Required preprocessing.** Tile generation at 512² with Macenko stain normalisation on H&E inputs and per-channel percentile clipping on IF/IHC outputs. Latent caching for the VAE pathway. CONCH patch coordinates aligned with the diffusion output grid via sliding-window inference and Hann-window blending.

### Model

The diffusion backbone is **FLUX.1-Kontext-dev**, a 12-billion-parameter Diffusion Transformer with 19 joint and 38 single blocks, hidden dimension 3072, 24 heads, 16 latent channels, and rotary positional embeddings. Conditioning is image-to-image via sequence-wise concatenation of reference (H&E) and target (IHC) latent streams, the native Kontext setup. Marker identity is supplied via a fixed text prompt and a 1-of-7 learnable embedding added to the timestep stream.

The 12-billion-parameter base, T5-XXL and CLIP-L text encoders, and the FLUX VAE are all frozen. The transformer is adapted via LoRA at rank 32 (alpha 32, dropout 0). Training proceeds for 30 epochs across the combined ORION + BCI corpus (approximately 80 000 optimizer steps on four A100 GPUs) using 8-bit AdamW (β = 0.9, 0.999; weight decay 0.01), learning rate 1 × 10⁻⁴ with 500-step linear warmup, and bf16 mixed precision throughout. Inference uses flow-matching Euler with 28 steps and a guidance scale of 2.5, the upstream Kontext default; a 50-step DPM-Solver++ run is reported in the appendix as a sampler-robustness check.

Sampling resolution is 512² for COSMO+ evaluation (matching the CONCH patch coarsening); a 1024² rendering with tile blending is produced as visualisation only.

### Baselines

Non-diffusion comparisons retrain on the same ORION + BCI corpus:
- **CycleGAN** (Zhu et al., 2017) — unpaired stain translation baseline.
- **Pix2pix-HD** (Wang et al., 2018) — paired baseline at 1024².
- **StainGAN** (Shaban et al., 2019) — single-marker GAN baseline.
- **VirtualMultiplexer** (Pati et al., 2024) — the published state-of-the-art for virtual multiplex IF, retrained from the authors' public code.

Diffusion comparisons:
- **PathLDM** (Yellapragada et al., CVPR 2024) — text-conditioned latent diffusion for histopathology, adapted with our marker prompts.
- **BCI-Diff / IHCDiff** (Yan et al., MICCAI 2024) — diffusion variant of the BCI baseline, retrained on ORION + BCI.
- **ASCS** (Liu et al., MICCAI 2024) — adaptive supervised diffusion for IHC translation.

Within-recipe ablations include a LoRA rank sweep (8 / 16 / 32 / 64), a marker-conditioning ablation (unconditional vs marker-embedding vs marker-text-prompt), and the reasoning-substrate ablation that defines the primary outcome: COSMO+ trained on (i) H&E channel only, (ii) virtual IHC channel only, and (iii) H&E concatenated with virtual IHC.

### Evaluation

**Primary outcome.** Δ IoU@10 between H&E-grounded COSMO+ (Aim 1's headline checkpoint) and virtual-IHC-grounded COSMO+ retrained with the same recipe. Averaged across 16 TCGA cohorts × at least 200 spatial-grounding questions per cohort.

**Secondary metrics.** Δ Concept Grounding Score, Δ Reasoning Fidelity Score, per-cohort Hallucination Rate, and diffusion-side fidelity controls (FID-50k per marker, MS-SSIM, biomarker-IoU at Otsu threshold) so that any downstream IoU@10 drop can be attributed to the reasoning rather than to trivially poor diffusion outputs.

**Pathologist validation.** Two gastrointestinal pathologists and one breast pathologist read 50 cases per cancer × three cancers (colorectal, breast, lung). Random presentation order, real-or-generated label hidden. A 5-point Likert scale rates the diagnostic plausibility of the virtual IHC view and the clinical correctness of the COSMO+ chain that cites it. Cross-rater κ is reported alongside per-cancer aggregated Likert.

**Downstream task evaluation.** The COSMO+ pipeline *is* the downstream task. Aim 3 is a controlled ablation on the spatial-grounding reward channel, with the Aim 2 interpretability harness providing all four metrics.

### Compute and storage

| Component | A100 GPU-hours | H100 GPU-hours |
|---|---|---|
| Diffusion fine-tune on ORION + BCI (30 epochs, 4 GPU DDP, bf16) | ≈ 133 | ≈ 75 |
| TCGA virtual-IHC inference (~1.2 M tiles × 28 sampling steps) | ≈ 30 | ≈ 17 |
| COSMO+ ablation retrain on virtual-IHC patch tokens (16 cohorts) | ≈ 20 | ≈ 11 |
| **Total Aim 3** | **≈ 180** | **≈ 100** |

Fits within approximately six wall-clock days of the 22-day buffer at 32-GPU parallelism.

**GPU type preference.** A100 80 GB or H100 80 GB SXM5. The 12 B FLUX backbone in bf16 at per-GPU batch 2 peaks at 64-73 GB; lower-VRAM cards do not fit.

**Storage delta.** Approximately 500 GB beyond the main project's 1 TB scratch ask (ORION already mirrored on cluster; BCI preprocessed tiles plus cached VAE latents plus virtual-IHC inference outputs).

### Deliverables

1. A virtual-IHC evaluation atlas covering 16 TCGA cohorts × representative markers × 50 slides per cohort × ≈ 1 000 tiles per slide, released on Zenodo (CC-BY 4.0) keyed by TCGA slide ID.
2. Marker-conditional LoRA adapters and a merged multi-marker checkpoint, released on Hugging Face Hub under the Kempner Institute / Yu lab namespace.
3. A per-cancer evaluation report styled identically to the Aim 1/2 result tables (cancers as rows, metrics as columns, mean ± standard deviation across folds), with an appendix tabulating baseline comparisons.
4. An optional dashboard panel appended to the Aim 2 dashboard: side-by-side H&E ↔ virtual-IHC overlay with a clickable cancer / slide selector.

### Risk and fallback

- **Cross-stain domain shift to TCGA.** Joint training on ORION (same-section) and BCI (serial-section) is the primary mitigation. If domain shift is still fatal, fall back to per-cancer small-set adaptation drawn from each TCGA cohort's diagnostic notes.
- **DDP numerical instability at the chosen learning rate.** Warmup is already extended and a documented lr = 1 × 10⁻⁶ fallback is in place. Throughput, memory, and utilisation measurements are unaffected by gradient finiteness.
- **TCGA inference exceeds the buffer.** Report on a pre-registered sampled subset (≈ 300 000 tiles, ≈ 8 GPU-hours). The hypothesis is testable on the subset.
- **Hard fallback.** If FLUX.1-Kontext does not converge on the joint corpus, fall back to a CycleGAN baseline as the virtual IHC channel. Report as a negative result with the cross-stain question still answered for GAN-grade virtual IHC. Manuscript scope shrinks but the falsifiable test still completes.

## Aim 4 — IHC-as-pseudo-label upgrade for tumor-marker subtype reasoning

### Scientific framing

**Working title.** *IHC-conditioned chain-of-thought traces lower COSMO+ hallucination rate on tumor-marker subtype questions.*

**Falsifiable hypothesis.** Augmenting Aim 1's CONCH text-similarity pseudo-label stream with virtual-IHC-derived marker-positive region pseudo-labels (produced by the Aim 3 diffusion model) lowers COSMO+ Hallucination Rate on three pre-registered marker-conditional question pools — HER2 in breast (TCGA-BRCA), PD-L1 in lung (TCGA-LUAD), and MMR/MSI in colorectal (TCGA-COAD + TCGA-READ) — by at least 25 % relative compared with the Aim 1 CONCH-only baseline, at paired-bootstrap p < 0.01, without statistically harming Hallucination Rate on non-marker-conditional questions in the same cohorts.

**Link to Aim 1 / Aim 2.** Direct upgrade of Aim 1's pseudo-label pipeline. The Aim 1 reward stack has four signals (format, diagnostic, spatial, concept); Aim 4 introduces a fifth — **marker-conditional grounding** — only for the three cancer-marker subtype subsets. The metric used to evaluate Aim 4 is Aim 2's Hallucination Rate, ensuring continuity of measurement across all four aims.

**Gap in the literature.** Tumor-marker subtype questions are an active hallucination failure mode of every published whole-slide vision-language model (SlideChat, WSI-LLaVA, PathChat-Plus). Real IHC slides are not always available at scan time; virtual IHC is the only scalable way to inject marker-level evidence into the reasoning pipeline. No prior work measures the effect of *virtual* IHC pseudo-labels on a vision-language model's hallucination rate, which is what Aim 4 supplies.

### Data

**Diffusion training.** Aim 4 reuses the Aim 3 marker-conditional weights; no additional diffusion training is performed.

**Question pools (pre-registered).** Approximately 200 questions per marker per cohort, extracted from the WSI-Bench and SlideBench-VQA-TCGA splits by regular-expression filtering on marker names (HER2 / ERBB2; PD-L1 / CD274; MMR / MSI / microsatellite / MLH1 / MSH2 / MSH6 / PMS2). A matched control pool of 200 non-marker-conditional questions per cohort (anatomy, grade, tumor type) is used to verify that improvements on the marker pool do not come at the cost of regressions elsewhere.

**Ground truth source for marker status.** TCGA-CDR Pan-Cancer Clinical Data Resource (Liu et al., *Cell* 173, 400–416, 2018) supplies HER2 status and MMR/MSI status. PD-L1 status is drawn from CPTAC-LUAD where available (≈ 40 % cohort coverage); the remainder is annotated by trained pathology readers on TCGA-LUAD diagnostic slides.

**Data security level.** DSL 1 throughout. TCGA-CDR is public; CPTAC-LUAD is public; the diagnostic-slide PD-L1 read is internal to the pathologist consortium and reports only counts and proportions.

### Model

**Diffusion backbone.** Frozen Aim 3 weights.

**Pseudo-label pipeline upgrade.** For each TCGA tile, virtual-IHC inference produces the marker-specific output relevant to the question (HER2 in BRCA, PD-L1 in LUAD; for MMR/MSI in COAD/READ we use the immune-microenvironment proxy described in the risk section below). Otsu thresholding yields marker-positive regions, which become the marker-conditional pseudo-labels feeding a fifth reward signal in the Aim 1 reward stack.

**COSMO+ training schedule.** One additional epoch of curriculum supervised fine-tuning on the marker-conditional question subsets, with the marker-grounding reward enabled. The COSMO+ architecture and LoRA rank are unchanged from Aim 1; the only new element is the additional reward-signal channel.

### Baselines

- **CONCH-only pseudo-label baseline** (Aim 1 as published). This is the head-to-head comparison for the falsifiable test.
- **Real-IHC pseudo-label oracle**, where real IHC is available (BCI subset of TCGA-BRCA and CPTAC-LUAD with PD-L1 reads). Upper bound: the virtual-IHC method should close at least 50 % of the gap between CONCH-only and real-IHC.
- **Random-mask control.** Random marker-positive regions of matched area distribution are injected as sham pseudo-labels. If the random control also lowers Hallucination Rate, the effect would be a reward-regularisation artefact rather than a marker-information effect.
- **Adversarial virtual-IHC control.** Virtual IHC is generated for the wrong marker (for example, PD-L1 conditioning when the question is about HER2). The Aim 4 effect should disappear if Hallucination Rate is genuinely sensitive to marker identity.

### Evaluation

**Primary outcome.** Δ Hallucination Rate (Aim 2 definition) on marker-conditional questions, with at least 25 % relative reduction required at paired-bootstrap p < 0.01.

**Secondary metrics.** Δ Concept Grounding Score on the same questions; Hallucination Rate on the control pool (must not increase); marker-status classification AUROC as a single-number summary of "does the model actually know HER2 / PD-L1 / MMR status now?"; expected calibration error of model confidence versus accuracy with and without the marker-conditional pseudo-label channel.

**Pathologist validation.** Three readers (breast, gastrointestinal, thoracic), each reading 30 cases per marker for 90 reads per pathologist. Reads cover both virtual-IHC diagnostic plausibility (Likert) and clinical correctness of the COSMO+ reasoning chain that cites the IHC view. Cross-rater κ reported per marker.

**Downstream-task evaluation.** This Aim is the downstream evaluation — Aim 4 closes the loop from Aim 1's reward signals through Aim 3's diffusion model to Aim 2's hallucination metric.

### Compute and storage

| Component | A100 GPU-hours | H100 GPU-hours |
|---|---|---|
| Virtual-IHC inference for ~30 000 marker-conditional tiles (3 cohorts × 3 markers) | ≈ 25 | ≈ 14 |
| COSMO+ curriculum-SFT extra epoch on marker-conditional subsets (~50 000 Q/A) | ≈ 70 | ≈ 38 |
| Baseline and ablation passes (CONCH-only, random-mask, adversarial) | ≈ 30 | ≈ 17 |
| **Total Aim 4** | **≈ 120** | **≈ 65** |

**GPU type preference.** A100 80 GB or H100 80 GB SXM5. The diffusion-inference component shares the Aim 3 memory envelope. The COSMO+ retraining component is on the Qwen2.5-7B + LoRA stack and tolerates 48 GB cards if necessary.

**Storage delta.** Approximately 50 GB additional for marker-conditional virtual-IHC tiles and reward-stream caches.

### Deliverables

1. Marker-conditional pseudo-label tables for the three marker × cancer pairs, released as a Zenodo archive paired with the question IDs from SlideBench-VQA-TCGA and WSI-Bench (CC-BY 4.0).
2. Three cancer-specific COSMO+ LoRA checkpoints incorporating the marker-grounding signal, released alongside the Aim 1/2 checkpoints.
3. A per-cancer × per-marker evaluation report styled identically to the Aim 1/2 result tables, with a paired-bootstrap confidence-interval column for the primary Hallucination Rate delta.
4. An optional marker-conditional reasoning view appended to the Aim 2 dashboard: for any HER2 / PD-L1 / MMR question, the dashboard overlays the cited marker-positive regions on the H&E view.

### Risk and fallback

- **Virtual-IHC quality on markers not directly in ORION.** HER2 is supplied by BCI; PD-L1 is in ORION's 13 channels. MMR/MSI has no direct IHC equivalent in our diffusion training data; we substitute an immune-microenvironment proxy based on the FOXP3 / CD8a / CD45 mix that distinguishes MSI-high tumors. This proxy is a documented compromise. If the proxy underperforms, Aim 4 is reported with HER2 and PD-L1 as primary endpoints and MMR/MSI as a stretch goal.
- **Statistically null Hallucination Rate delta.** The primary endpoint and the three-marker analysis plan are pre-registered to prevent post-hoc cherry-picking. If the pooled delta is null but a single-marker delta is significant, the report says so explicitly.
- **Reward over-fitting to diffusion-model failure modes.** The real-IHC oracle ablation detects this directly: if oracle and virtual agree, the signal is sound; if they disagree, the threshold is recalibrated or the marker is dropped from the primary table.
- **Hard fallback.** A negative result on Aim 4 is publishable as evidence that virtual-IHC marker grounding does not transfer to a vision-language reasoner; Aim 3 then carries the virtual-staining contribution. The marker-conditional question is deferred to a follow-up R21 / F-series award.

## Suggested Abstract additions for full four-Aim coverage

> Aim 3 asks whether the spatial chain-of-thought reward survives a change of stain modality: a FLUX.1-Kontext diffusion model is trained on paired ORION colorectal multiplex immunofluorescence and BCI breast HER2 immunohistochemistry data, then applied zero-shot to TCGA H&E to produce virtual IHC channels over which COSMO+ is re-evaluated. Aim 4 augments the Aim 1 reward stack with a fifth, marker-conditional pseudo-label channel derived from the same virtual IHC, and tests whether this lowers COSMO+ hallucination rate on tumor-marker subtype questions (HER2 in breast, PD-L1 in lung, MMR/MSI in colorectal) by at least 25 percent relative. Both secondary aims share the diffusion backbone, fit inside the 22-day buffer at roughly 300 combined A100 GPU-hours, and use only public DSL-1 data.
